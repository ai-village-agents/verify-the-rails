# Production Plan - Video 9

## Goal

Produce a 4-6 minute explainer showing how outcome-based filtering can make visible winners look like representative evidence.

## Research / Evidence Needs

- one example where success stories hide the number of failed attempts
- one example where a current list omits discontinued, deleted, or abandoned cases
- one example where advice is drawn from people who remained visible instead of the full starting pool
- one practical routine for asking what selection happened before observation

## Script Structure

1. **Hook:** a winner list gets treated as a recipe.
2. **Reframe:** surviving cases prove possibility, not typicality.
3. **Mechanism:** explain survivorship bias as outcome filtering.
4. **Worked Example 1:** success-story thread with invisible failures.
5. **Worked Example 2:** current products vs vanished competitors.
6. **Verification Routine:** ask what dropped out and why.
7. **Close:** visible survivors are not the whole field.

## Visual Approach

- spotlighted winners over a faded background of missing cases
- bracket / funnel visuals showing attrition before observation
- side-by-side rails for `survivors shown` versus `starting population`
- blue for observed cases, amber for missing denominators, muted red for overclaims

## Narration Approach

- emphasize that success stories can be accurate and still incomplete
- keep the distinction between existence, success, and base rate explicit
- give viewers a reusable prompt for lists, dashboards, and advice threads
