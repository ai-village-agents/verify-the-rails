# Video 1 Brief

## Working Goal

Create a flagship introduction for curious non-experts that explains why conflicting web claims are often caused by infrastructure and summarization drift, not only misinformation.

## Title Options

1. **Two True Screenshots, One Wrong Conclusion**
2. **Why the Same Web Claim Looks Different to Different People**
3. **How Good Sources Still Disagree Online**

## Chosen Title

**Two True Screenshots, One Wrong Conclusion**

## Viewer Promise

By the end of this video, viewers will be able to diagnose at least three common reasons credible-looking web claims disagree and apply a simple verification routine before sharing.

## Core Thesis

Conflicting web claims are often explainable. If you check source type, timestamp, and delivery path, you can separate real disagreement from stale or transformed information.

## Target Length

8-10 minutes

## Section-by-Section Outline

1. **Cold Open (0:00-0:45)**
   - Show two conflicting screenshots of the "same" claim.
   - Ask: "How can both look authentic?"

2. **What Viewers Usually Assume (0:45-1:45)**
   - Common assumptions: one side is lying; one image is fake.
   - Reframe: sometimes both captures are real and out of sync.

3. **Mechanism 1: Source-vs-Live Drift (1:45-3:15)**
   - Explain primary source updates versus copied summaries.
   - Show a timeline of claim changes.

4. **Mechanism 2: Caching + Deployment Lag (3:15-5:15)**
   - Plain-language explanation of CDN/app rollout delay.
   - Show how different viewers can receive different versions briefly.

5. **Mechanism 3: Summary Errors (5:15-6:45)**
   - How headline compression and AI/secondary summaries introduce drift.
   - Distinguish paraphrase from direct quote.

6. **Verification Routine (6:45-8:30)**
   - Five-step checklist:
     - Find primary source
     - Check timestamp/time zone
     - Confirm page freshness (reload, hard refresh, alt network)
     - Compare exact wording
     - Mark uncertainty before sharing

7. **Close (8:30-9:15)**
   - Restate core idea: disagreement is often diagnosable.
   - Invite viewers to apply checklist on next conflicting claim.
