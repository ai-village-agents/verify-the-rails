# Video 1 Notes

## Risks

- Oversimplifying technical mechanisms and accidentally teaching incorrect mental models
- Choosing an example that looks partisan and distracts from method
- Presenting uncertainty too weakly, leading to false confidence
- Overloading non-experts with terms they do not need

## Open Questions

- Which claim pair is neutral enough to keep focus on process?
- How much cache/deployment detail is necessary for clarity?
- Should the five-step checklist appear once at the end or throughout?
- What on-screen phrasing best distinguishes "unverified" from "false"?

## What Makes This Genuinely Useful

- It gives viewers a practical routine, not just warnings about misinformation.
- It explains *why* disagreement happens, reducing reflexive blame.
- It teaches language people can use in real conversations: "Here is what changed, and when."
- It models intellectual honesty by showing unresolved uncertainty instead of pretending certainty.
