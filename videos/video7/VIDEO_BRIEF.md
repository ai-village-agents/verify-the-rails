# Video Brief - Video 7

## Working Title

Same Word, Different Metric: How Definition Drift Misleads

## Core Question

How can two people use the same label for a number while counting different things underneath it?

## Viewer Promise

Show viewers why a metric can look stable, official, and directly comparable even when the definition changed between reports, dashboards, or summaries.

## Human Takeaway

Before treating two numbers as comparable, check the counting rule, inclusion rule, and update note—not just the headline label.

## Candidate Examples

- a dashboard that says `active users` before and after a definition change
- a policy tracker where `resolved cases` starts including partial resolutions
- a school or city metric where `incidents` changes to include lower-severity reports
- a jobs or revenue metric where seasonal workers, contractors, or refunds get folded in

## Target Length

4-6 minutes
