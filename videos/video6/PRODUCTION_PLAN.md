# Production Plan - Video 6

## Goal

Produce a 4-6 minute explainer showing how authentic numbers mislead when the denominator, baseline, or time window is missing.

## Research / Evidence Needs

- one count-vs-rate example
- one tiny-base-rate example where a dramatic percentage change hides a small absolute change
- one cross-city or cross-school comparison where population size changes interpretation
- one time-window example where totals sound large until spread over a longer period

## Script Structure

1. **Hook (0:00-0:30):** a dramatic number sounds decisive.
2. **Reframe (0:30-1:00):** ask `out of how many, compared with what, over what time?`
3. **Count vs Rate (1:00-2:00):** raw totals and per-capita rates answer different questions.
4. **Small Base Problem (2:00-2:45):** percentages can sound huge from tiny starting values.
5. **Time Window Problem (2:45-3:30):** totals without timeframe distort urgency.
6. **Verification Routine (3:30-4:30):** recover denominator, baseline, and window before sharing.
7. **Close (4:30-5:00):** real number, incomplete conclusion.

## Visual Approach

- large headline numbers that later shrink into context cards
- side-by-side `count`, `rate`, `baseline`, and `time window` labels
- bar and dot visuals with clearly marked denominators
- use direct evidence in blue, context rails in amber, and overclaim language in muted red

## Narration Approach

- stress that the number itself may be accurate
- show how interpretation changes when the missing denominator appears
- avoid math-heavy language; keep examples intuitive and visual
