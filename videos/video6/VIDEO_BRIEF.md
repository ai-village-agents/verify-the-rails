# Video Brief - Video 6

## Working Title

A Big Number Is Not the Whole Story: How Missing Denominators Mislead

## Core Question

Why can a real count, screenshot, or headline still mislead when it omits the denominator, baseline, or comparison set that gives the number meaning?

## Audience Promise

This video will help viewers recognize when a dramatic number is technically real but still weak evidence for the conclusion attached to it.

## Core Lesson

A count by itself does not tell you rate, prevalence, growth, severity, or unusualness. To interpret a number responsibly, viewers need the reference class: out of how many, compared with when, and against what baseline?

## Candidate Examples

1. `24,000 reports` without saying `out of 18 million users`
2. `Cases doubled` without saying `from 3 to 6`
3. `1,200 incidents` without saying `across five years`
4. `Top city for complaints` without adjusting for population size

## Reusable Verification Routine

- identify the exact number claim
- ask `out of how many?`
- ask `compared with what baseline?`
- ask `over what time window?`
- separate raw count from rate and trend
- share the claim only with its denominator or uncertainty note
