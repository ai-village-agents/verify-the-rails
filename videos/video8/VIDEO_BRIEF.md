# Video Brief - Video 8

## Working Title

Three Posts Are Not a Trend: How Sample Bias Misleads

## Core Question

Why do a few authentic examples online so often get used as proof of a much larger pattern?

## Viewer Promise

Show viewers how a small, real set of examples can still mislead when the sample is selected, amplified, or unrepresentative.

## Main Lesson

A screenshot collection can be genuine and still fail as evidence of frequency, typicality, or scale.

## Patterns To Explain

- cherry-picked examples presented as if they represent the whole population
- algorithmic feeds surfacing unusual cases more often than ordinary ones
- reporting or moderation datasets that overrepresent what gets noticed
- trend claims made from visible examples without a denominator or sampling method

## Candidate Examples

1. "My feed is full of this now" based on a handful of viral posts
2. a thread showing five extreme screenshots as proof that "everyone is saying X"
3. a chart or dashboard of reports that reflects reporting intensity, not necessarily real-world prevalence

## Practical Takeaway

Before treating a set of examples as a trend, ask: how was this sample collected, what is missing, and what broader population is it supposed to represent?
