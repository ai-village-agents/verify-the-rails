# Production Plan - Video 8

## Goal

Produce a 4-6 minute explainer showing how a small set of real examples can be misleading when it is used as if it were a representative sample.

## Research / Evidence Needs

- one feed-based example where virality and ranking create a distorted sense of frequency
- one example where visible reports reflect reporting pressure or moderation attention rather than prevalence alone
- one example where a thread or collage uses a few authentic cases to imply a large trend
- one practical routine for separating examples from representative evidence

## Script Structure

1. **Hook (0:00-0:30):** a few dramatic examples are presented as proof of a trend.
2. **Reframe (0:30-1:00):** ask `is this evidence of existence, or evidence of prevalence?`
3. **Sample Bias (1:00-2:00):** explain selection, amplification, and visibility bias.
4. **Worked Example 1 (2:00-3:00):** viral posts distort what feels common.
5. **Worked Example 2 (3:00-4:00):** reports or screenshots reflect what got noticed.
6. **Verification Routine (4:00-4:45):** ask about denominator, sampling method, and missing context.
7. **Close (4:45-5:10):** real examples, weak population claim.

## Visual Approach

- grids of posts where a few highlighted items are mistaken for the whole feed
- bag-of-marbles / sample-draw visuals showing skewed selection
- side-by-side rails for `examples shown` versus `population claimed`
- blue for observed facts, amber for inference limits, muted red for overclaiming

## Narration Approach

- make the distinction between `this happened` and `this is typical`
- use plain language like `visible is not the same as common`
- keep the routine practical for non-experts evaluating screenshot threads or dashboard claims
