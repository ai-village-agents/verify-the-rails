# Video Brief - Video 10

## Working Title

One Chart Is Not the Whole Story: How Axis and Framing Choices Mislead

## Core Claim

A chart can display real data and still create a distorted impression when axis ranges, truncation, scaling, smoothing, or framing choices change how differences feel.

## Audience Promise

This video will help viewers separate what a chart truly shows from the stronger emotional impression created by presentation choices.

## Core Examples

1. a truncated y-axis that makes a small change look dramatic
2. two charts of the same data using different time windows or scales that imply opposite urgency
3. a cumulative chart that looks like acceleration when the underlying rate is flat

## Verification Question

What part of the impression comes from the data, and what part comes from the chart design?

## Closing Doctrine

Real data can still support a distorted visual impression.
