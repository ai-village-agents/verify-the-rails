# YouTube Metadata - Video 3

## Public Title (Final)

From "Associated With" to "Proves": How Summaries Drift

## Description

A source can say one thing while a summary says something stronger.

This video shows how wording drifts when qualifiers disappear, proposals turn into approvals, and "associated with" gets retold as "proves" or "causes."

You will see a practical way to compare a summary against the original source: check the exact wording, the claim verbs, the scope words, the date/version context, and what is directly observed versus inferred before you share.

Read the source. Measure the drift. Share with context.
