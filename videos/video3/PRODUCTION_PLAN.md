## Research / Evidence Needs

- One clear source document and one stronger secondary summary of that same source.
- Exact wording captures from both, with timestamps and publication dates visible.
- At least one example each of drift by stronger wording, omitted qualifiers, and outdated context.
- A short uncertainty note for any interpretation that is inferred rather than directly stated in the source.

## Script Structure

1. **Hook (0:00-0:40):** source says one thing, summary says a stronger thing.
2. **Reframe (0:40-1:20):** not every summary is wrong; the task is to test wording strength against source text.
3. **Source Hierarchy (1:20-2:05):** source document > derived summary > repost commentary.
4. **How Drift Happens (2:05-3:15):** compression, omission, simplification, outdated context, and wording that outruns the source.
5. **Worked Comparison (3:15-4:45):** side-by-side exact phrasing with observed vs inference labels.
6. **Verification Routine (4:45-5:55):** practical repeatable steps viewers can run quickly.
7. **Close + Checklist (5:55-6:35):** reusable checklist and scoped conclusion.

## Visual Approach

- Match Video 1 and Video 2 visual language: clean editorial graphics, restrained motion, strong legibility.
- Repeated side-by-side format: `Source Wording` vs `Summary Wording` with highlighted verb shifts.
- Persistent certainty tags on claims: `Observed`, `Inference`, `Unknown`.
- Timeline/date strip on examples so context age is always visible.

## Narration Approach

- Calm, specific, fair-minded, and evidence-first.
- Plain-English definitions for terms like "associated," "proposal," and "approved."
- Keep a strict sequence: state observed text first, then inference, then confidence.
- Avoid cynicism and avoid absolutes; emphasize scope limits and uncertainty where needed.
