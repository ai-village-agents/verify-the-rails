# Video Brief - Video 5

## Working Title

The Crop Hides the Clue: How Missing Context Changes Screenshot Claims

## Viewer Promise

Show viewers how a screenshot can contain real pixels but still mislead when the crop removes the domain, date, filter, version label, banner, or surrounding text that determines what the image actually proves.

## Core Problem

People often treat screenshots as self-contained evidence. But a crop can remove exactly the contextual clue that changes the meaning: whether the page is a draft, whether the chart is filtered, whether the update is current, whether the site is official, or whether a warning banner is visible just off-screen.

## Candidate Examples

1. **Chart filter crop**
   - visible crop says `24,000 users`
   - hidden filter says `Last 24 hours` rather than `All users`
2. **Status page banner crop**
   - screenshot shows `Investigating`
   - cropped-out top banner says `Resolved 20 minutes later`
3. **Document crop**
   - quoted paragraph is real
   - hidden header says `Draft for review`
4. **Domain crop**
   - screenshot looks official
   - hidden address bar or footer reveals mirror/repost domain rather than primary source

## Key Lessons

- a screenshot is evidence of what was visible inside the frame, not everything around it
- missing context is not the same as fake evidence, but it limits what can be concluded
- before sharing, ask what is outside the crop as well as inside it
- domain, date, version, filter, and banners are evidence rails

## Reusable Checklist

1. identify the exact claim the screenshot is being used to support
2. look for missing context: domain, date, filter, version, status banner, surrounding labels
3. compare against the uncropped source if possible
4. mark what the screenshot directly shows versus what depends on hidden context
5. share with a confidence label or uncertainty note

## Tone / Constraints

- calm and specific
- avoid claiming that every crop is deceptive or malicious
- distinguish incomplete evidence from fabricated evidence
- keep examples legible and practical for non-experts
