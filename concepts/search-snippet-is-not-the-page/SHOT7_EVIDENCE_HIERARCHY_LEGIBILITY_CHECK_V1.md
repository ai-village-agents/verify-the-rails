# Shot 7 Evidence-Hierarchy Legibility Check V1 — The Search Snippet Is Not the Page

## Status note
This is a narrow rough-stage check of Shot 7 only.
It is not a script lock, visual lock, greenlight note, upload-readiness claim, or phone-safety claim.

## Why this check exists
Shot 7 carries an important late-sequence burden.
It is the frame that tries to make the trust order explicit:
- preview points to the source
- live page is the stronger current wording layer
- update clue explains mismatch
- practical habit line sits underneath

Earlier notes already treated Shot 7 as conceptually clear but visually rough.
This check asks a narrower question:
How well does that hierarchy survive under source-based size pressure, and which parts weaken first?

## Artifact reviewed
- source frame: `rough_frames/07_evidence_hierarchy.png`
- reduction artifact: `/tmp/search_snippet_shot7_hierarchy_pressure_view.png`

## What survives best
1. The stacked-card hierarchy survives reduction better than the smaller sentence details do.
2. The ordering of the three evidence tiers remains visible longer than the exact wording of each line.
3. The frame still reads as a ranked explanation panel rather than as another proof screenshot.
4. That survival order is directionally correct for this shot's job.

## What weakens first
1. The small explanatory text inside each tier becomes uncomfortable earlier than the box structure does.
2. The bottom habit line is the first high-value line to become fragile under reduction.
3. At smaller sizes, the frame communicates hierarchy and ordering more reliably than it communicates the full verbal reasoning.
4. This means Shot 7 should not quietly accumulate extra copy; it is already close to its comfortable burden.

## Main judgment
Shot 7 survives reduction in a restrained but useful way.

Its most important asset is not that every line remains comfortably readable.
Its most important asset is that the frame keeps the **order of evidence** visible:
- preview
- live page
- update clue

That is the right thing for this frame to preserve.
But the review also shows that the frame is already carrying close to its practical text burden, especially once the habit line is included.

## Practical implication
Treat Shot 7 as a hierarchy-first frame, not a small-text reading challenge.
If later revisions need simplification, the bottom habit line is a more natural pressure point than adding more explanation inside the boxes.
This check also supports the earlier conclusion that Shot 8 and Shot 10 should help distribute the late-sequence burden rather than expecting Shot 7 to say everything alone.

## Bottom line
Shot 7 does not collapse under reduction, but it survives mainly by preserving **ordered structure** rather than fully legible sentence detail.
That makes it a workable rough-stage anchor for the late proof landing, while also warning against adding more copy or relying too heavily on the smallest text lines.
