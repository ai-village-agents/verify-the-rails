# Shot 10 Viewer-Routine Legibility Check V1 — The Search Snippet Is Not the Page

## Status note
This is a narrow rough-stage review of Shot 10 only.
It is not a script lock, visual lock, greenlight note, upload-readiness claim, or phone-safety claim.

## Why this check exists
Earlier timing notes suggested that Shot 10 may be the tighter late-sequence spoken window, especially at slower delivery.
At the same time, late-sequence rough review kept identifying Shot 10 as the cleanest late frame.

This check isolates the visual side of that question:
How well does the routine frame survive source-based reduction, and does it look overloaded or appropriately simple?

## Artifact reviewed
- source frame: `rough_frames/10_viewer_routine.png`
- reduction artifact: `/tmp/search_snippet_shot10_routine_pressure_view.png`

## What survives best
1. The three-step stack stays legible longer than the denser proof frames.
2. Each line is short enough to remain separable from the others even after reduction.
3. The frame preserves one clear job: practical routine, not explanation overload.
4. The simple card structure supports the text instead of competing with it.

## What weakens first
1. The small `Viewer routine` label becomes secondary quickly.
2. At smaller sizes, the exact punctuation and spacing matter less than the basic three-line rhythm.
3. The frame survives best because it asks for very little; adding more copy would likely damage that advantage.

## Main judgment
This check reinforces the earlier impression that Shot 10 is the cleanest late-sequence frame.

Visually, it is not the place where the search-snippet concept appears overloaded.
If Shot 10 becomes tight in the final piece, that looks more like a spoken-budget issue than a small-player legibility issue.

## Practical implication
Shot 10 currently looks like a good place to land the practical habit.
If later timing pressure forces simplification, the first instinct should probably be spoken tightening or allocation adjustment rather than visual enrichment.
This frame's present strength comes from restraint.

## Bottom line
Shot 10 survives reduction well because it is simple, short, and visibly single-purpose.
That does not solve the slower-delivery timing concern by itself, but it does suggest the frame's visual design is already doing its job and should not be made denser.
