# Storyboard Expansion Decision V1 — The Search Snippet Is Not the Page

## Status note
This is a scope decision note after the rough seven-frame prototype pass.
It is not a visual lock, script lock, greenlight note, or upload-readiness claim.

## Question
Does the current seven-frame rough prototype justify expanding this concept into a fuller storyboard?

## Short answer
Yes, but only to the next planning layer.

The concept now has enough structural evidence to justify a fuller storyboard pass.
It does **not** have enough finish evidence to justify production, greenlight language, or upload-readiness claims.

## Why expansion is now justified
The rough prototype has cleared the main structural question that was still open:
- whether a direct wording-compare step could exist without collapsing into a text wall

Current evidence now supports all of the following:
1. **Frame 1** can make the preview feel sufficient on purpose.
2. **Frame 4** can make the qualifier feel like the decisive meaning shift.
3. **Frame 5** can directly compare wording if the shared text is calmed down and the differing phrases are isolated and enlarged.
4. **Frame 6** can work better once the wording mismatch is already established.
5. **Frame 7** can land the hierarchy and habit in a compact, non-scolding way.

That is enough to say the concept has a viable proof spine rather than just an interesting premise.

## Why expansion should still stay narrow
The current prototype still has important limits:
1. **Frame 5 remains the most text-dependent frame.**
2. **Frame 3 is still more setup-like than vivid.**
3. **Frame 6 still benefits from narration more than the other key frames do.**
4. **Finish quality is unproven.**

So the correct next move is not a broad production push.
The correct next move is a controlled expansion that preserves what the rough prototype actually proved.

## What a justified expansion should include
A fuller storyboard pass is justified **if** it does all of the following:
- keeps the main contradiction centered on one synthetic example
- preserves the strongest visible sequence:
  - preview confidence
  - qualifier shock
  - wording compare
  - update clue
- treats **Frame 4** as the conceptual hinge rather than diluting it
- uses **Frame 5** as explicit proof, not as the emotional center
- keeps hierarchy / habit language short and routine
- avoids adding dense explanatory blocks just because more shots are available

## What expansion should not mean
Expanding the storyboard should **not** be treated as evidence that the concept is:
- visually locked
- script-locked
- greenlit
- upload-ready
- safe to judge for phone performance yet

It also should not trigger:
- a broad narration rewrite
- decorative interface detailing that increases reading burden
- stronger implied claims about indexing mechanics than the synthetic example justifies

## Proposed next-step shape
The most defensible next storyboard pass would likely:
1. keep the existing rough seven-frame proof spine intact
2. expand around it only where extra shots add clarity rather than density
3. use extra shots mainly for:
   - opening confidence
   - layer transition
   - reusable closing habit
4. delay any second-pattern flash until after the main contradiction is already solved cleanly

## Current decision
**Proceed to fuller storyboard planning, cautiously.**

Best current label:
- **structurally promising enough to expand**
- **still prototype-grade overall**
