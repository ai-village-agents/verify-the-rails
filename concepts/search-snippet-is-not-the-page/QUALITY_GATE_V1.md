# Quality Gate V1 — The Search Snippet Is Not the Page

## Status note
This is a planning checkpoint for concept quality.
It is not a greenlight, upload recommendation, or proof that the concept is ready.

## Purpose
This gate exists to prevent the concept from advancing just because the lesson sounds sensible.

The piece should only move forward if it proves that:
1. the disagreement is concrete
2. the visual structure teaches the lesson clearly
3. the viewer leaves with a reusable habit

## Scoring categories
Score each category from 1 to 5.

### 1. Hook strength
Question:
- Does the opening feel specific and surprising enough to justify attention?

Target:
- The opening should create a real contradiction: the preview is real, the page is real, but the conclusion is still too broad.

### 2. Visual storytelling
Question:
- Can the main lesson be understood from visible layer shifts rather than narration alone?

Target:
- The concept should work through preview -> page -> update clue structure, not explanatory text blocks.

### 3. Distinctiveness
Question:
- Does this feel like a Verify the Rails piece rather than generic advice to read more carefully?

Target:
- The disagreement should be artifact-based, time-aware, and evidence-ranked.

### 4. Viewer value
Question:
- Does the viewer get a practical checking habit they can reuse immediately?

Target:
- The habit should be short, concrete, and usable by non-experts.

### 5. Example integrity
Question:
- Is the central synthetic example internally consistent across wording, timing, and UI logic?

Target:
- The mismatch should be believable without depending on hand-wavy platform behavior.

### 6. Small-player resilience
Question:
- Will the core meaning survive if fine print gets lost?

Target:
- The qualifier, update clue, and layer labels must remain structurally legible without tiny explanatory copy.

### 7. Pacing potential
Question:
- Does the structure suggest a clean 2.5 to 4 minute piece without repetitive restatement?

Target:
- The concept should resolve fast once the update clue appears.

### 8. Finish potential
Question:
- Does the concept look likely to support polished execution rather than prototype-only clarity?

Target:
- The surfaces should offer clean transitions, restrained labels, and a strong closing callback.

## Minimum gate
- No category below **3/5**
- Target at least **30/40** overall
- Must score at least **4/5** in:
  - Hook strength
  - Visual storytelling
  - Viewer value

## Non-negotiables
The concept does **not** pass if any of the following are true:
- The disagreement only works because the viewer patiently reads a long explanation block.
- The preview/page difference is too subtle to show clearly.
- The piece collapses into generic “read the article” advice.
- The example depends too heavily on one real platform’s exact behavior.
- The update clue is decorative instead of explanatory.
- The concept requires claiming certainty about platform indexing behavior beyond what the synthetic example can honestly show.

## What would count as a strong pass
A strong pass would look like:
- an opening contradiction that feels immediate
- a central example that remains coherent under scrutiny
- visual logic that makes the evidence hierarchy obvious
- a short closing habit the viewer can remember
- likely readability without tiny support microcopy carrying the whole lesson

## What would count as a weak pass or cautionary pass
Signs of caution even if the concept is promising:
- the lesson works in prose better than in pictures
- the qualifier only matters once the viewer reads dense text
- the example is believable but emotionally flat
- the update clue arrives too late or feels like paperwork
