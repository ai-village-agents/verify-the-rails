# Frame Planning Note V1 — The Search Snippet Is Not the Page

## Status note
This is a planning-stage frame note.
It is not a visual lock, storyboard lock, greenlight note, or upload-readiness claim.

## Purpose
Pressure-test the concept at the frame level before any renderer work.
The question is not whether the lesson sounds right in prose.
The question is whether the key meaning survives in a few visible moments without relying on tiny explanatory text.

## Core frame test
The concept probably lives or dies on three moments:
1. the preview feels sufficient
2. the live-page qualifier clearly narrows the claim
3. the update clue explains the mismatch

If any one of those moments is weak, the piece risks collapsing into narration-dependent explanation.

## Recommended central wording pair
Use a synthetic policy example with a short enough wording delta to compare cleanly.

Preview layer:
- `Refunds are available for all annual plans.`

Live page layer:
- `Refunds are available for annual plans within 14 days of purchase.`

Update clue:
- `Updated March 14, 2026`

## Key frame roles

### Frame role A — Preview confidence
Goal:
- make the preview feel quotable and conclusive

Must be visible at a glance:
- one highlighted snippet line
- enough surrounding search-result context to read it as a preview layer

Should not require:
- multiple result cards
- dense side explanation

### Frame role B — Qualifier reveal
Goal:
- make the narrowing phrase do obvious meaning work

Must be visible at a glance:
- the live page sentence
- emphasis on `within 14 days of purchase`

Preferred treatment:
- dim the unchanged shared wording
- keep the qualifier phrase brightest
- avoid long arrows or paragraph callouts

Failure mode:
- if the viewer has to read the full sentence carefully to detect the difference, Shot 4 is too weak

### Frame role C — Update clue
Goal:
- explain why preview and page can both be real

Must be visible at a glance:
- one strong date cue
- clear association with the live page

Preferred treatment:
- isolated update chip or page header row
- amber or other decisive accent reserved for the date cue

Failure mode:
- if the date feels like metadata garnish, the disagreement will seem arbitrary

### Frame role D — Side-by-side compare
Goal:
- show scope change without a text wall

Preferred compare structure:
- left: preview sentence
- right: live page sentence
- short labels only, such as `preview` and `current page`
- single contrast emphasis on the missing vs added scope

Failure mode:
- if the compare frame needs several explanatory bullets, the concept is getting too dense

### Frame role E — Evidence hierarchy card
Goal:
- leave the viewer with a reusable trust order for present-tense claims

Suggested labels:
- `Preview: pointer`
- `Live page: current wording`
- `Update clue: explains mismatch`

## Small-player protection rules
- Prefer one bright qualifier phrase over several medium-emphasis phrases.
- Prefer one date cue over a row of tiny metadata.
- Prefer short labels over sentence-length callouts.
- Keep the compare frame narrow enough that both sentences can stay large.
- Avoid making the page body look like a full article; it is a policy block, not an essay.

## Early visual pass criteria
A rough frame pass is promising only if:
1. the preview line reads instantly
2. the qualifier phrase reads instantly
3. the update date reads instantly
4. the side-by-side compare does not feel crowded
5. the lesson is understandable with the sound off at a coarse level
