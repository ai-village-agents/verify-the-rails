# Narration Beat Timing V1 — The Search Snippet Is Not the Page

## Status note
This is a planning-stage timing map.
It is not a script lock, visual lock, greenlight note, or upload-readiness claim.

## Why this exists
The current script is now stable enough to treat as a working baseline.
A small timing map helps test whether the spoken beats and storyboard beats still fit together without adding more narration.

## Timing philosophy
This concept works best if it stays compact and visibly proof-driven.
That means timing should favor:
- enough room for Shot 4 to feel decisive
- enough room for Shot 5 to read at a glance
- enough room for Shot 6 to explain the mismatch
- very little room for Shot 9

The goal is not exact seconds yet.
It is a practical first distribution that keeps the strongest proof beats central.

## Working timing map
### Beat 1 — Hook
**Script**
`This preview was real. The page was real. The mistake was treating the preview as the page.`

**Likely shots**
- Shot 1 leading into Shot 2

**Rough timing target**
- 5–7 seconds

**Why**
The line is short and strong.
It should land quickly without being rushed.

### Beat 2 — Preview confidence
**Script**

**Likely shots**
- Shot 1 into Shot 2

**Rough timing target**
- 6–8 seconds

**Why**
This needs enough time for the preview to feel convincing before the contradiction begins.

### Beat 3 — Live-page reveal
**Script**

## Rough whole-piece implication
Using the ranges above, the concept still looks comfortable in a short-explainer envelope.
A likely practical outcome is something around 60–75 seconds depending on pauses, visual dwell, and whether Shot 9 stays in.

## Main timing priorities to preserve
1. give Shot 4 enough room to feel like the hinge
2. let Shot 5 prove the wording mismatch without becoming a text wall
3. keep Shot 6 explanatory but not bloated
4. keep Shot 9 brief enough that it does not dilute the first example
5. end quickly once the viewer routine and callback have landed

## Bottom line
The current script and storyboard still look timing-compatible.
The main remaining timing risk is not overall length.
It is local allocation: making sure the piece spends its seconds on the hinge and proof beats, not on explanation spillover.
