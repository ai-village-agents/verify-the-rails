## Frame-by-frame currentness-cue load

### Shot 6 / `06_update_clue`
This is the strongest direct currentness carrier in the late proof chain.
It contains all three ingredients at once:
- the live page surface
- the narrower live wording
- the dated update clue (`Updated March 14, 2026`)

Interpretation:
- if the viewer is going to absorb **why the page outranks the preview for the current claim**, Shot 6 does the heaviest lifting
- this is the most explicit bridge from mismatch to recency

### Shot 7 / `07_evidence_hierarchy`
This is the strongest **compressed restatement** of the currentness logic.
It does not show the date itself, but it explicitly labels:
- `Preview: points to the source`
- `Live page: current wording`
- `Update clue: explains mismatch`

Interpretation:
- Shot 7 already converts the dated evidence from Shot 6 into a reusable rule
- the phrase `Live page: current wording` is the clearest compact currentness cue available after the direct date beat
- this matters because the shortened fallback drops the spoken word `present-tense`

### Shot 8 / `08_pattern_label`

### Shot 10 / `10_viewer_routine`
This frame contains only the routine:

Interpretation:

### Shot 11 / `11_closing_callback`
This frame closes with:

Interpretation:
- Shot 11 is a source-vs-preview callback, not a currentness explainer

## Narrow synthesis
The late visuals do appear to carry a **plausible currentness chain** before Shot 10:
1. Shot 6 provides the explicit dated update clue on the live page
2. Shot 7 compresses that into the label `Live page: current wording`
3. Shot 8 repeats the pattern in smaller form while pivoting toward the lesson
4. Shot 10 then converts the lesson into a routine

That means the shorter fallback would **not** be operating in a visual vacuum.
The currentness idea has already been visually planted before the routine begins.

## Important limit
The support is **indirect by Shot 10**, not direct.
The key caveat is:
- the visuals likely make `the page is stronger evidence` more defensible than timing-only analysis suggested
- but the visuals do **not** fully erase the editorial difference between
  - `stronger present-tense evidence`
  - and `stronger evidence`

The shorter line would still be less explicit in isolation about **current-state priority**.
The visual chain only reduces that risk; it does not remove it.

## Current judgment
Most defensible planning-stage read right now:
- the shorter fallback has a better visual-support case than before
- specifically because Shot 6 and Shot 7 already carry the currentness logic into the late landing
- however, the support is strongest **before** Shot 10, not inside Shot 10 itself
- so this is still not a reason to rewrite the line now

## Practical implication
If a later spoken review makes `present-tense evidence` feel too abstract,
then `the page is stronger evidence` would now have two bounded supports behind it:
1. it improves Shot 10 / Shot 11 timing ownership
2. the surrounding late visuals plausibly preserve enough currentness context to keep the meaning legible

## Bottom line
The shorter fallback is now better supported than before,
but still as a **prepared fallback**, not as a justified immediate script change.
