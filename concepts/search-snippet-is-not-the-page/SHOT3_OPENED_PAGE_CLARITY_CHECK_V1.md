# Shot 3 Opened-Page Clarity Check V1 — The Search Snippet Is Not the Page

## Status note
This is a narrow rough-stage review of Shot 3 only.
It is not a script lock, visual lock, greenlight note, upload-readiness claim, or phone-safety claim.

## Why this check exists
Earlier review accepted the calmer Shot 3 because it improved source-page identity and sequence discipline.
But that judgment had not yet been pressure-tested with a source-based reduction view.

That matters because Shot 3 is doing a specific setup job:
- show that the viewer has opened the source page
- make the layer change from search preview to live page legible
- avoid stealing the main narrowing reveal from Shot 4

## Artifact reviewed
- source frame: `rough_frames/03_live_page.png`
- reduction artifact: `/tmp/search_snippet_shot3_clarity_pressure_view.png`

## What survives well
1. The frame still reads clearly as an opened page rather than another search result.
2. The browser chrome plus inner content surface continue to do useful identity work.
3. The `Refund policy` heading survives reduction better than the smaller supporting text.
4. The calmer treatment helps the frame feel like a setup beat instead of a second claim card.

## What weakens first
1. The smaller policy sentence becomes uncomfortable earlier than the page identity does.
2. The update-date line weakens quickly and should not be treated as the main proof payload of this shot.
3. At smaller sizes, the frame communicates “opened help-center page” more reliably than it communicates the exact wording on the page.

## Main judgment
This is a useful confirmation of the earlier calmer-pass decision.

Shot 3 survives reduction in the way it most needs to:
- the live/source-page identity persists
- the frame still feels distinct from the search-preview layer
- the shot remains appropriately setup-oriented rather than hinge-oriented

The frame does **not** survive as a strong small-size reading beat for all its text details.
But that is acceptable because the main burden of qualifier shock still belongs to Shot 4, not Shot 3.

## Practical implication
Treat Shot 3 as a clarity-and-transition beat, not a detail-heavy proof beat.
Its job is to establish that the page has been opened and that the viewer is now looking at the source layer.
If later revisions try to make Shot 3 carry too much exact-text burden, this check argues against that move.

## Bottom line
Shot 3 is not one of the strongest standalone frames, but its calmer opened-page treatment holds up better than a purely prose review might suggest.
The shot survives reduction mainly by preserving source-page identity and transition clarity, which is the right burden for this beat.
