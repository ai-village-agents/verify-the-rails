# Opening Options V1 — The Search Snippet Is Not the Page

## Status note
This is a planning document for hook exploration.
It is not a script lock, storyboard lock, or greenlight note.

## Goal of the opening
The opening should create an immediate contradiction:
- the preview is real
- the page is real
- the mistake is treating the preview as the page itself

The opening should also make the viewer curious about **how two honest-looking receipts can disagree**.

## Option 1 — Strongest current line
**“This preview was real. The page was real. The mistake was treating the preview as the page.”**

Why it works:
- clean paradox
- short enough to stage quickly
- introduces layer confusion directly
- fits the Verify the Rails tone

Risk:
- if the visuals are weak, the line could still sound slightly abstract

## Option 2 — Broader-claim version
**“The search result said it plainly. The page did too — until the qualifier changed what ‘plainly’ meant.”**

Why it works:
- points toward wording scope rather than simple contradiction
- prepares the viewer for a qualifier reveal

Risk:
- a little more literary and less instantly visual than Option 1

## Option 3 — Disagreement-first version
**“One person quoted the search result. Another opened the page. They looked like they were arguing about facts. They were really arguing about layers.”**

Why it works:
- starts from a recognizable social pattern
- frames the piece as disagreement diagnosis

Risk:
- slightly slower and more explanatory
- may delay the paradox too much for the first seconds

## Option 4 — Present-tense trap version
**“A preview can quote something true and still give you the wrong current answer.”**

Why it works:
- highly reusable viewer lesson
- immediately introduces time-sensitivity

Risk:
- feels more like a thesis sentence than a cold open
- may work better as an early follow-up line than the first line itself

## Recommended opening structure
Best current sequence:
1. show the search result snippet first
2. deliver Option 1 or a close variant
3. open the page and reveal the qualifier
4. reveal the update clue
5. then explain the disagreement

This order preserves the emotional logic:
- confidence first
- destabilization second
- explanation third

## What the opening should not do
- Do not begin with generic advice like “always check the source.”
- Do not begin with platform criticism.
- Do not spend the first seconds explaining caching, indexing, or search infrastructure.
- Do not make the viewer read multiple small text blocks before the contradiction lands.
- Do not oversell certainty about exactly why a preview lagged if the piece is using synthetic examples.

## Current preference
Current best lead remains **Option 1** because it is the most compact statement of the core paradox and depends the least on extra explanation.

## Current judgment
Promising opening territory, but still planning-only.
