# Late Sequence Plan V2 — The Search Snippet Is Not the Page

## Status note
This is a planning-stage late-sequence note.
It is not a visual lock, script lock, greenlight note, upload-readiness claim, or phone-safety claim.

## Why this update exists
`LATE_SEQUENCE_PLAN_V1.md` captured the right broad restraint principle:
- keep the ending smaller than the proof chain
- reuse already-earned visual language
- avoid turning the ending into a second explainer block

But later timing and legibility checks made the older rough timing numbers too loose.
This V2 keeps the same overall philosophy while updating the timing posture to match the current evidence more closely.

## Current main judgment
The late sequence should remain compact, calm, and subordinate to the proof chain.
Its job is still:
- label the mistake briefly
- optionally widen it once only if the beat stays tiny
- hand the viewer a practical routine
- close cleanly

The safest supported ending posture is now more specific than before:
- Shot 7 anchors
- Shot 8 stays brief
- no Shot 9 by default
- Shot 10 lands the routine
- Shot 11 closes quietly

## What later checks changed
Later notes added four important constraints:
1. The no-Shot-9 rough animatic already supports a workable ending around `61.04s`.
2. Shot 9 can fit only as a very brief flash and does not create meaningful narration capacity.
3. Shot 10 is visually the cleanest late frame, so it is the safest place to let the routine breathe.
4. The current trust-order line is already the shortest measured option, so late-sequence timing pressure should not be treated as a synonym problem.

## Sequence principle
The ending should feel like a taper from the main proof chain, not a rebuild of momentum.
That means:
- Shot 8 is a label beat, not a new argument
- Shot 9 is optional and should remain absent unless a very small flash clearly helps
- Shot 10 and Shot 11 remain the real spoken landing

Earlier planning allowed a looser 3–5 second shape.

## Shot 10 — Viewer routine
### Recommended job
Leave the viewer with a reusable checking habit in the simplest possible shape.

### Best current routine
1. `Open the page.`
2. `Compare the wording.`
3. `Check one update clue.`

### Practical timing target
- about **6–7s** as the baseline
- if a later spoken pass shows real tightness, allow about **0.5–1.0s** more here before bloating other late beats

### Why this timing changed
Later alignment checks showed Shot 10 is the late spoken window most likely to feel slightly tight at slower delivery.
That does not mean the frame is weak.
It means this is the first late beat that deserves extra room if any small reallocation becomes necessary.

### Restraint rule
Do not turn the routine into miniature narration captions.
Protect its memorability, not its completeness.

1. keep Shot 9 cut
2. compress Shot 8 toward the low end of its range
3. only then consider cautious trims elsewhere

Avoid treating Shots 7 or 11 as the first source of recovered time, because both already sit close to their spoken loads.
Avoid weakening Shot 6 casually, because it still carries the main explanatory block in the proof chain.

## What this plan does not claim
This note does not claim that the late sequence is fully validated.
It only updates the safest current planning posture so the broad late-sequence plan matches the newer measurement stack.

## Bottom line
The ending should still stay smaller than the proof chain that precedes it.
What has changed is not the philosophy, but the specificity:
- Shot 8 should be brief
- Shot 9 should usually stay absent
- Shot 10 is the main practical landing and the first justified recipient of any small extra late time
- Shot 11 should close quietly without recap
