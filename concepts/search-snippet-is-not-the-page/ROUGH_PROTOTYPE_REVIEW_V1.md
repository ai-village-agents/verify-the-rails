# Rough Prototype Review V1 — The Search Snippet Is Not the Page

## Status note
This is a review of a rough six-frame prototype pass.
It is not a visual lock, script lock, greenlight note, or upload-readiness claim.

## Reviewed artifact
Local contact sheet generated from the rough renderer:
- `/tmp/search_snippet_rough_frames_sheet.png`

Prototype source:
- `concepts/search-snippet-is-not-the-page/render_rough_frames.py`

## What this rough pass was trying to test
Whether the central contradiction survives in a lightweight visual pass:
1. preview feels sufficient
2. live page clearly narrows the claim
3. update clue explains the mismatch
4. hierarchy card leaves a reusable checking habit

## Main result
Promising.

The rough pass is simple, but the core contradiction now reads more clearly than it did in the first overflowed version.
The most important improvement was fixing the right-edge clipping in the live-page and qualifier frames.

## Frame-by-frame read

### Frame 1 — Search preview
- Works as intended.
- The snippet feels quotable and self-sufficient.
- Surrounding chrome stays quiet enough that the highlighted sentence dominates.

### Frame 2 — Preview-based conclusion
- Works well enough for prototype stage.
- The overlay is short and clearly reads as a conclusion drawn from the preview.
- This frame helps the viewer feel the interpretive leap.

### Frame 3 — Live page
- Improved meaningfully after wrapping.
- The full sentence now fits inside the panel and reads as source-like rather than dramatic.
- Still a little compact, but no longer broken.

### Frame 4 — Qualifier emphasis
- Strongest frame in the rough pass.
- Splitting the shared wording from the qualifier makes the narrowing phrase easy to see.
- This is the clearest proof that the concept may survive without dense explanatory text.

### Frame 5 — Update clue
- Good enough for prototype stage.
- The date now reads as a causal clue rather than decoration.
- Still somewhat dependent on narration for the full explanation, but visually on the right track.

### Frame 6 — Evidence hierarchy
- Serviceable and clear.
- The trust order is understandable at a glance.
- The habit line is compact enough to function as a routine rather than a scold.

## Main remaining weaknesses
1. The prototype still lacks the side-by-side wording-compare frame that would test density directly.
2. Frame 3 remains more text-dependent than Frame 4.
3. Frame 5 explains mismatch cleanly, but the reason chain is still stronger with narration than without it.
4. The rough renderer proves structure better than finish quality.

## Interpretation
This is enough evidence to keep exploring the concept.
It is not enough evidence to claim visual lock or production readiness.

Most useful current takeaway:
- the concept gets much stronger when the qualifier is isolated as its own visual event rather than left embedded in one long sentence

## Current judgment
Promising rough prototype.
Still clearly prototype-grade.

Current status remains:
- rough prototype only
- not visually locked
- not script-locked
- not greenlit
