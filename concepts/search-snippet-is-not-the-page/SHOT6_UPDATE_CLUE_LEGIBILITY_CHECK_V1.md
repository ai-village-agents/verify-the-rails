## Visual review takeaways
### 1. The frame survives structurally better than the smallest text details do
At reduced sizes, the viewer can still recognize:
- a page card
- a highlighted date chip in the upper right region
- a centered block of policy wording

So the frame keeps its broad role as a live page plus update clue.

### 2. The exact update date is the most fragile high-value detail
At 100 percent and 75 percent, the date chip remains readable enough for review.
At 50 percent, the chip still reads strongly as an update marker, but the exact date becomes much less comfortable.
At 35 percent, the chip remains visible mainly as a highlighted clue shape rather than reliably readable date text.

### 3. The frame therefore supports the clue best as a visibly marked update, not as tiny date text doing all the work alone
The rough composition is better at signaling:
- there is an update clue here
than at guaranteeing:
- every viewer will effortlessly read the full absolute date at smaller sizes

That means the date chip still benefits from narration and sequence context.
It should not be treated as independently robust tiny-text proof.

### 4. This is still better than a fully narration-only clue
Even when the exact date becomes less comfortable, the highlighted chip remains a clear visual event.
That matters because the frame does not collapse into generic page filler.
The viewer can still tell that an update marker is present and important.

## Main judgment
This check supports a restrained conclusion:
- Shot 6 appears strong enough to carry an update-clue role in the rough sequence
- but the exact absolute date is still one of the first details to weaken under size pressure
- so Shot 6 should be treated as a mixed visual-plus-narration proof beat, not as a tiny-text self-sufficient frame

## What this changes
This does not change project status.
It does slightly refine the current risk statement:
- Shot 6 is not failing structurally
- the risk is narrower than total narration dependence
- the vulnerable detail is the exact date readability, not the existence of an update clue at all

## Bottom line
The current rough Shot 6 frame keeps the update clue visible as a highlighted event even when reduced.
But the exact date text degrades before the frame's overall structure does.
So the safest interpretation remains:
- Shot 6 can support the proof chain
- the sequence should still help the viewer understand why the update clue matters
- no small-player or phone-safety claim is justified from this check
