The draft script reinforces the existing proof spine rather than replacing it. It keeps the visual center of gravity where it should be:

- the page quietly disagrees
- the qualifier changes scope

That is the right direction for this concept.

## What is working
### 1. The opening still fits the concept exactly
`This preview was real. The page was real. The mistake was treating the preview as the page.`

This remains the clearest verbal statement of the whole piece and matches Shots 1–2 well.

### 2. The script does not over-explain infrastructure
The wording about a preview keeping `older wording, or shorter wording` is cautious enough for a synthetic example and does not force a detailed claim about exact search-system mechanics.

### 3. The script preserves Frame 4 as the hinge
The draft gives the narrowing phrase room to matter instead of trying to narrate past it.
That is important because the qualifier reveal is still the strongest single visual beat.

### 4. The closing routine matches the evidence hierarchy
`Open the page. Compare the wording. Check one update clue.`

This works as a compact habit and matches the proven three-layer trust order.

## Remaining risks
### 1. One line is still somewhat abstract
`the page is stronger present-tense evidence`

The idea is correct, but the exact phrasing may sound slightly conceptual in read-through. This is a future timing/ear test issue, not a reason for broad rewriting now.

### 2. The script still depends on Shot 5 and Shot 6 doing their jobs
If the compare step becomes dense or the update clue feels decorative, the script should not grow to compensate. The right fix would still be visual restraint and clarity.

### 3. The script does not yet cover a full Shot 9 second-pattern flash
That is acceptable for now. The main proof chain is more important than forcing early generalization.

## Current judgment
The current script is aligned enough with the storyboard to justify later timing or read-through tests.
It should be treated as a promising baseline, not a locked script.
