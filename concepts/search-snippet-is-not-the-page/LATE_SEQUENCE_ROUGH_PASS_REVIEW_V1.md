## Frame-by-frame read

### Frame 08 — Pattern label
**Current read**
The frame successfully reuses the hierarchy language from Shot 7.
The sentence
`The mistake was treating the preview as the page.`
reads like a label placed onto an existing conclusion rather than the start of a new argument.

**Why that is useful**
This is exactly the safer role for Shot 8.
It means the beat can likely function as categorization instead of explanation spillover.

**Remaining caution**
The frame is not trying to out-compete Shot 7 visually, which is correct.
That also means its value depends on sequence placement more than standalone punch.
That is acceptable at this stage.

---

### Frame 10 — Viewer routine
**Current read**
This is the cleanest of the new late-sequence frames.
The three-line routine is compact, legible, and easy to remember:
1. `Open the page.`
2. `Compare the wording.`
3. `Check one update clue.`

**Why that is useful**
The frame keeps the routine in the same calm visual world as the hierarchy frame without turning into a checklist lecture.
It supports the idea that Shot 10 can remain simple.

**Remaining caution**
This still has not been pressure-tested for pacing inside a full timed sequence.
But visually, the restraint choice looks right.

---

### Frame 11 — Closing callback
**Current read**
The frame clearly echoes the preview-card silhouette from the opening.
That helps the closing line
`A preview can point you to a source. It is not the source.`
feel like corrected recognition rather than a final twist.

**Why that is useful**
The visual callback keeps the ending tied to the original premise instead of drifting toward recap graphics or extra proof.

**Remaining caution**
This frame already carries enough text by rough-stage standards.
That does not make it unusable.
It just means the safest future direction is to avoid adding any more support copy here.
