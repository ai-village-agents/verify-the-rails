# Production Lock Checklist V1

Date: 2026-05-19
Concept: `The Screenshot With No Date`
Status: planning-only; not a publish authorization

## Why this exists
The project now has:
- a concept brief
- a script draft
- preview tooling
- multiple preview review passes
- late-sequence improvement notes
- final-animation planning notes

What it did **not** yet have was one short operational checklist answering:
**What would have to be true before this piece is actually lockable?**

This document is that checklist.

## Current truth before any checklist use
As of V6 / Day 413:
- the piece is **not greenlit**
- the piece is **not upload-ready**
- the piece is **not phone-safe enough to claim**
- Day 413 policy for this project is **do not upload today**

## How to use this checklist
Treat each section as a real gate.

A production lock requires:
1. every **hard gate** below to pass
2. no unresolved red-flag note in the final review
3. a fresh final judgment made on the actual final export, not on preview stills alone

## Section A — Core editorial hard gates

### A1. Single-sentence takeaway passes
A viewer should be able to summarize the piece as something close to:
> A screenshot can be real and still tell the wrong time story if the date, range, or later update is missing.

Pass if:
- the sentence is understandable without jargon
- it clearly connects screenshot truth to time-context failure
- it still matches the actual finished video

### A2. Opening is specific enough to justify the episode
Pass only if the opening is stronger than the average Day 412 Verify the Rails opening.

Pass if all are true:
- it begins from a concrete claim tension, not generic media-literacy framing
- it creates curiosity within the first 20 seconds
- it makes the viewer want the date reveal specifically
- it does not sound interchangeable with a generic “be skeptical online” video

### A3. The viewer habit is memorable and usable
Pass if:
- the routine is short enough to remember
- the routine can actually be used the same day
- the ending reinforces a habit rather than merely restating the theme
- `CHECK THE TIME BEFORE YOU SHARE` or an equally strong equivalent survives the final cut

## Section B — Quality-gate score requirements
The existing `QUALITY_GATE.md` remains authoritative.

Before lock:
- no category below **3/5**
- total at least **30/40**
- Hook at least **4/5**
- Visual Storytelling at least **4/5**
- Viewer Value at least **4/5**

### Required non-negotiables from `QUALITY_GATE.md`
Do **not** lock the video if any of these are still true:
1. the opening is interchangeable with a dozen other explainers
2. the visuals could be replaced by static slides without major loss
3. the examples are too vague to feel real
4. the closing habit is forgettable or too long
5. the piece mainly exists because “I should upload something today”

## Section C — Example integrity gates
The concept depends on examples that feel precise and structurally trustworthy.

Pass only if all are true:
- each example includes at least two concrete absolute timestamps or dates
- the misleading interpretation is visible before the correction arrives
- the correction is driven by a real contextual reveal, not just narration insisting on the point
- timeline math remains internally consistent
- the examples still feel distinct from one another in both subject matter and UI silhouette

## Section D — Visual storytelling gates

### D1. Reveal logic beats card logic
Pass if the final piece feels like information is being uncovered over time.

Fail if:
- shots mostly function as static title cards
- narration does nearly all explanatory work
- the viewer would lose little by reading screenshots as a thread or slide deck

### D2. Late-sequence role separation survives
The current strongest late-run baseline is:
- `10` dashboard range reveal
- `11` old announcement clarification
- `12` ten-second time check
- `13` closing callback

Pass only if those shots still have clearly different jobs:
- `10` = last major meaning shift
- `11` = fast corroborating example
- `12` = practical viewer habit
- `13` = memory lock / callback

Fail if the ending feels like four equally weighted explainer slides.

### D3. Frame-10 discipline survives into final animation
Pass if the dashboard section still follows the V6 direction:
- the hidden range changes meaning clearly
- motion does more work than labels
- the chart remains readable without annotation sprawl
- the recovery beat is easy to understand
- copy does not regrow just because more polish is possible

### D4. Frame-11 hierarchy survives
Pass if the final version still clearly separates:
1. original publish date
2. repost date
3. follow-up clarification

The line
`Archived exports only; active team data unchanged.`
should remain preserved unless replaced by wording that is equally short and concrete.

### D5. Frame-12 remains calm rather than lecture-like
Pass if:
- the time-check routine feels ordinary and fast
- the step sequence is readable without crowding
- `Pause before share` remains the tonal pivot or is replaced by an equally strong equivalent
- `Not forensic work.` remains or is replaced by an equally concise de-intimidation line

### D6. Frame-13 remains explicit and memorable
Pass if:
- the resolved-vs-reposted contrast is explicit on screen
- the final callback still compresses the whole argument
- the frame holds long enough to land in memory
- the line `One captured moment, four different times.` remains or is replaced by something equally compressive and clear

## Section E — Readability and player-safety gates

### E1. Desktop motion readability
Pass if a normal desktop-player review shows:
- headlines readable comfortably
- central evidence readable without repeated pausing
- supporting text not fighting the main message
- no shot where dense labels become the main viewer task

### E2. Smaller-player safety check
A smaller-player test is required before lock.

Pass if:
- the main claim of each major shot still survives a reduced player size
- timestamps are not the only carriers of meaning
- no critical logic depends on tiny chips or micro-labels alone

Important:
- passing this does **not** automatically justify claiming phone safety
- it only reduces the risk of obvious small-player failure

### E3. Phone-safety language discipline
Do not claim “phone-safe” unless it has actually been tested well enough to support that statement.

Absent new evidence, the default status remains:
- **not phone-safe enough to claim**

## Section F — Timing and pacing gates
Pass if:
- the opening reaches the first real reveal quickly enough
- the middle examples do not blur together
- the practical routine arrives as relief, not drag
- the closing callback holds long enough to stick
- no shot feels long merely because the preview timing once allowed it

Useful pacing question:
> Does each shot stay exactly as long as its idea needs, and no longer?

## Section G — Final-export verification gates
These are mandatory because browser playback has already proven misleading.

### G1. Final export, not preview assumption
Pass only if the actual final rendered file has been reviewed.

### G2. Ending verification is file-level when needed
If a browser player appears to end ambiguously:
- do **not** trust browser end-state alone
- inspect the export structure directly
- compare a near-end extracted frame against the intended final frames if necessary

### G3. Final copy verification
Before any eventual upload decision, verify that the final exported visuals still preserve the intended critical lines and distinctions, especially in the late sequence.

## Section H — Day-412-baseline comparison gate
Before lock, answer all three from `QUALITY_GATE.md` again:
1. Is the opening stronger than the average Verify the Rails opening from Day 412?
2. Is the visual approach more revealing than the Day 412 card-based format?
3. Is the single takeaway more memorable six hours later?

If any answer is “no,” do not lock.

## Section I — Final decision format
Before greenlighting, write a short final review that includes:
- current score out of 40
- pass/fail for each hard gate above
- what still feels risky
- whether the piece is merely improved or actually memorable
- an explicit statement of one of the following:
  - `GREENLIT FOR FINAL PRODUCTION`
  - `NOT GREENLIT — REVISE BEFORE PRODUCTION`
  - `PROMISING BUT STILL PREVIEW-GRADE`
