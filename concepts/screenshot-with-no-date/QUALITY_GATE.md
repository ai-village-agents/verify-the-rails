# Quality Gate — The Screenshot With No Date

This is a pre-publish gate, not a post-hoc rationalization. If the concept does not clear this bar, do not release it.

## Scoring method
Score each category from 1 to 5.
- **1** = weak / not ready
- **3** = acceptable but unremarkable
- **5** = clearly strong

Target:
- no category below **3**
- total target **30/40** or higher
- at least **4/5** on Hook, Visual Storytelling, and Viewer Value

## Categories
### 1. Hook
Does the first 20 seconds create immediate curiosity and make the viewer want the reveal?

### 2. Clarity of core idea
Can a viewer summarize the point in one sentence after watching?

### 3. Visual storytelling
Do the visuals reveal information over time rather than merely decorate narration?

### 4. Distinctiveness
Does this feel like a specific, memorable piece rather than a generic media-literacy explainer?

### 5. Viewer value
Will an ordinary viewer leave with a useful habit or lens they can apply the same day?

### 6. Pacing
Does the video move with intention, using pauses and reveals well, without dragging?

### 7. Script quality
Is the language concrete, compressed, and free of unnecessary abstraction?

### 8. Finish / overall impression
Would I confidently say this is meaningfully better than the Day 412 baseline?

## Non-negotiable pass conditions
Even if the score is high, do not publish if any of these fail:
1. The opening is still interchangeable with a dozen other explainers
2. The visuals could be replaced by static slides without major loss
3. The examples are too vague to feel real
4. The closing habit is forgettable or too long
5. The piece mainly exists because "I should upload something today"

## Comparison test against Day 412 baseline
Before publishing, answer all three:
- Is the opening stronger than the average Verify the Rails opening from Day 412?
- Is the visual approach more revealing than the Day 412 card-based format?
- Is the single takeaway more memorable six hours later?

If any answer is "no," revise before release.
