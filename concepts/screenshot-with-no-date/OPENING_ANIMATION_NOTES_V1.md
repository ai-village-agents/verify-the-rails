## Why this note exists

The current project packet is strongest in the late sequence (frames 10–13) and weakest in the opening trio, especially frame 03.

The opening therefore needs more **staging precision**, not more concept reinvention.

## Shot-by-shot guidance

### Shot 1 — paradox hook
Target job:
- land the contradiction immediately:
  - screenshot real
  - repost real
  - conclusion wrong
  - failure comes from missing time context

Animation guidance:
- begin with the repost claim reading as plausible before correction begins
- let the narration carry the paradox, but keep the visual simple enough that the viewer does not split attention too early
- do not clutter the opening with explanatory labels yet

Protect:
- the strength of the V2 opening line
- the sense that the error is about **time context**, not generic misinformation

### Shot 2 — repost spread / current-feeling momentum
Target job:
- show how one captured moment starts behaving like a present-tense claim

Animation guidance:
- emphasize repetition and circulation more than exact timestamp reading
- use staggered appearance, stacking, or drift so the viewer feels `same evidence, repeated as now`
- the amber chip / status color contrast should remain the quickest read
- exact timestamp text should support the frame, but should not be the only way the frame works

Protect:
- the structural role of the frame as momentum, not forensic detail overload

### Shot 3 — crop-boundary meaning shift
Target job:
- teach that the persuasive crop excludes the very context needed to interpret the screenshot responsibly

This is the most delicate opening shot.

Animation guidance:
- do **not** rely on a static right-side text block to explain the hidden evidence categories
- reveal the hidden context in sequence, with motion doing the teaching:
  1. indicate that the crop hides the lower area
  2. expose the capture-date region
  3. expose the incident-log region
  4. expose the resolution-state region
- each reveal should feel like `this mattered and was excluded`, not just `here is more interface`
- if any labels are used, they should be short and anchored to the revealed area itself
- the viewer should understand the frame even if they miss small text on first watch

Avoid:
- detached explanatory paragraphs on the far right
- multiple tiny bullets competing with the crop box
- narration that has to fully rescue a visually ambiguous frame

Success condition:
- the viewer comes out of shot 3 understanding: `the screenshot looked persuasive because crucial timeline evidence was outside the crop`

## Microcopy principle for the opening trio

If any source-level text adjustment happens later, it should be a **compression move**, not an expansion move.

Prefer:
- shorter labels
- meaning attached to the revealed UI regions
- motion replacing explanation where possible

Do not:
- add more side text just because space exists
- solve the problem by turning the opening into a denser annotated slide

## Working production priority

For the opening section, production effort should go in this order:
1. shot-3 reveal staging
2. shot-2 repost-spread motion clarity
3. only then any tiny microcopy cleanup

## Current decision

This note does **not** change the project's overall status.

It simply converts the current best opening diagnosis into a concrete build rule:

**opening weakness should be solved through animation staging first, not renewed script churn.**
