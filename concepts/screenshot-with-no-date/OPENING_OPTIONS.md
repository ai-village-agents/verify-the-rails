# Opening Options — The Screenshot With No Date

Goal: test multiple openings before any production begins. The first 20 seconds should create curiosity, establish the problem clearly, and make the viewer want the reveal.

## Option 1 — Direct paradox
**Draft**
This screenshot is real. The repost is real. The conclusion people drew from it was wrong. Not because the image was fake — because the date was missing.

**Why it works**
- immediate contradiction
- simple language
- establishes the video's thesis fast

**Risk**
- slightly abstract unless paired with a concrete image very quickly

## Option 2 — Scenario-based
**Draft**
You see a screenshot that says a service is down. Everyone is reposting it. You cancel your plans, warn your friends, maybe get angry at the company — and then you find out the screenshot was from six hours ago.

**Why it works**
- easy to imagine
- human consequences are clear
- naturally leads into timestamp checking

**Risk**
- may feel narrow if the rest of the video broadens too quickly

## Option 3 — Progressive reveal
**Draft**
At first glance, this image seems to settle the story. Then you zoom out. A timestamp appears. Then a timezone. Then an update posted later. The screenshot didn't lie to you. It just arrived without enough context.

**Why it works**
- matches the visual strategy
- promises a reveal structure
- emphasizes missing context without sounding accusatory

**Risk**
- slower start if the visuals do not land immediately

## Option 4 — Habit challenge
**Draft**
Before you share a screenshot, ask one question: when exactly was this captured? That one question can turn a convincing post into a very different story.

**Why it works**
- instantly actionable
- compact and memorable
- frames the video as a useful skill

**Risk**
- less dramatic than the paradox opening

## Option 5 — Social proof / common behavior
**Draft**
A lot of the most misleading posts online use completely real screenshots. No photoshop. No fake quote. Just an image with the time context removed, cropped, or forgotten.

**Why it works**
- broad and credible
- connects to common online experience
- sets up multiple example types

**Risk**
- could sound generic if not followed by a vivid example

## Current ranking
1. **Option 1 — Direct paradox**
2. **Option 3 — Progressive reveal**
3. **Option 2 — Scenario-based**
4. Option 4 — Habit challenge
5. Option 5 — Social proof / common behavior

## Recommendation
Use **Option 1** as the current default, but storyboard it with a concrete status-page or dashboard screenshot immediately on screen. If the visuals can carry a slower reveal elegantly, re-test **Option 3**.
