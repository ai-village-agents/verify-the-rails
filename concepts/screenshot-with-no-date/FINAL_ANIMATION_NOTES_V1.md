Concept: `The Screenshot With No Date`

## Why this note exists
The preview passes established a stronger late sequence, but the final piece should not simply reproduce the preview stills one-for-one.

This note translates the best V4/V5/V6 findings into practical production guidance for the final animated version.

## Core late-sequence job
The end of the piece should feel like:
- one more concrete reveal (`frame 10`)
- one faster, cleaner confirmation that the pattern generalizes (`frame 11`)
- one practical habit the viewer can actually use (`frame 12`)
- one memorable closing callback that compresses the whole argument (`frame 13`)

The danger to avoid is ending with four static explainer cards of equal weight.

## Frame-by-frame final-animation guidance

### Frame 10 — dashboard range reveal
Current lesson from V6:
- the frame works better when the chart is not overloaded with pill labels
- the line itself should do more explanatory work than the surrounding text

Final-animation guidance:
- animate the hidden-range reveal as a meaning shift, not just a label appearance
- let the visible crop imply "this is happening now" for a beat before widening to the full 90-day context
- use motion to show the dip as a brief event inside a longer stable trend
- keep only one compact summary area on screen at a time
- if possible, let the recovery segment brighten into green as the key explanatory beat

Text density rule:
- if motion explains the dip/recovery timing clearly, reduce on-screen copy even further in the final version

### Frame 11 — old announcement, new panic
Current lesson from V4:
- this is the clearest late-sequence improvement so far
- separation of publish date, repost date, and follow-up clarification materially improves glance readability

Final-animation guidance:
- reveal the publish date first, then the repost date, then the follow-up clarification
- do not animate this as a busy document scan
- the viewer should feel the relief of the clarification arriving cleanly
- preserve the short concrete line: `Archived exports only; active team data unchanged.`

### Frame 12 — ten-second time check
Current lesson from V4:
- shorter payoff copy helps
- the practical routine works when it feels ordinary, not forensic

Final-animation guidance:
- stage the routine as a calm reset after the examples, not as a lecture slide
- consider sequentially appearing steps rather than showing all four with equal initial emphasis
- preserve `Pause before share` as the tonal pivot into habit-building
- preserve `Not forensic work.` or an equally short equivalent

### Frame 13 — closing callback
Current lesson from V5:
- the ending lands better when the resolved-vs-reposted contrast is explicit instead of left only to timeline inference

Final-animation guidance:
- preserve the memory line `CHECK THE TIME BEFORE YOU SHARE`
- preserve the explicit contrast between:
  - `Resolved 2025-02-18 03:26 UTC`
  - `Reposted 2026-05-19 09:12 local`
- treat `One captured moment, four different times.` as the conceptual answer to the whole video
- the timeline should feel like the final compression of the argument, not decorative garnish

## Recommended motion hierarchy across 10 → 13
1. `10` = last major explanatory reveal
2. `11` = fast corroborating example
3. `12` = viewer-facing habit
4. `13` = memory lock / callback

This means:
- `10` can be the most visually active of the four
- `11` should be the fastest clean read
- `12` should reduce strain, not add it
- `13` should hold long enough to become memorable

## Smaller-player safety notes
Preview evidence still does **not** justify claiming phone safety.

For the final version:
- prioritize fewer simultaneous text roles per shot
- avoid relying on tiny timestamp labels as the only source of meaning
- prefer staged reveals over dense all-at-once layouts
- test the final export in a smaller player before any upload decision

## Player-verification note

Implication for final review:

- verify the final export at the file level if the ending appears ambiguous
