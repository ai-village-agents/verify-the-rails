# Preproduction Brief — The Screenshot With No Date

Status: concept development only  
Date: 2026-05-19

## Working title options
1. **The Screenshot With No Date**
2. **A Real Screenshot Can Still Mislead You**
3. **The Missing Timestamp Problem**
4. **Before You Share That Screenshot, Check the Date**

## Core promise to the viewer
A screenshot can be completely authentic and still push you toward a false conclusion if the time context is missing, cropped, stale, or misunderstood.

## Why this concept is worth doing
This topic is:
- broadly useful outside AI Village context
- easy to grasp immediately
- highly visual
- compatible with richer pacing than the Day 412 batch
- a natural fit for Verify the Rails without repeating the exact same series formula

## Audience
Primary viewer:
- ordinary internet users who share screenshots from news sites, dashboards, status pages, social platforms, and messages

Secondary viewer:
- people who work in communications, support, journalism, education, or operations and need a simple habit for checking screenshot claims before resharing them

## What makes this better than the earlier batch format
Instead of rapidly moving through multiple examples with static cards, this piece should aim for:
- one sharp idea
- fewer but stronger examples
- more progressive visual reveals
- more attention to suspense and audience inference
- a cleaner emotional arc: certainty -> doubt -> method -> confidence

## Strong candidate opening
A viewer sees a screenshot that says a service is down, a policy changed, or a public figure posted something explosive. The screenshot is real. The repost is real. The problem is that the image is already old, and the missing date is doing all the persuasive work.

## Three-part structure
### 1. Hook: true image, wrong conclusion
Show a realistic screenshot that seems decisive. Let the viewer form an impression before revealing the missing timestamp.

### 2. Diagnosis: where time context hides
Explain the common places time context gets lost:
- cropped headers
- missing timezone
- old screenshots resurfacing
- cached or stale public pages
- screenshots of updates without the follow-up resolution

### 3. Habit: a repeatable verification move
Give a small routine:
1. Ask when this was captured
2. Find the original source page or account
3. Check timezone and update history
4. Look for what changed after the screenshot
5. Share only with time attached

## Example pattern shortlist
### Example A — Status incident screenshot
A screenshot of "Investigating" spreads widely after the issue is already marked resolved.

### Example B — dashboard metric screenshot
A screenshot shows a dramatic number, but the visible period is missing or cropped.

### Example C — quote or policy screenshot
A screenshot of an announcement is real, but it refers to an old draft, pilot, or earlier version.

### Example D — social post screenshot
A screenshot of a post is authentic, but the repost hides that it is months old or already corrected.

## Visual treatment ideas
### Visual principle
The visuals should make the viewer *feel* how context disappears.

### Specific techniques
- begin with a tight crop, then gradually zoom out
- animate a hidden timestamp appearing late
- build a simple timeline beneath the screenshot
- use side-by-side "what viewers infer" vs "what the evidence actually shows"
- distinguish **Observed / Inference / Unknown** visually with color and motion

## Audio / narration goals
- calmer, more deliberate pacing than the Day 412 videos
- allow short pauses after reveals
- avoid over-explaining obvious points
- keep language concrete and plainspoken

## Risks
- could become repetitive with Video 4 or Video 5 if framed too broadly
- could drift into generic media literacy advice
- could become slide-heavy again if the visual plan is not specified early

## Countermeasures
- make timestamp loss the explicit center of gravity
- use fewer examples and more reveal-based staging
- storyboard motion and framing before final script lock

## Success condition
If produced well, this should feel like a stronger flagship-style Verify the Rails episode: not just correct, but memorable.
