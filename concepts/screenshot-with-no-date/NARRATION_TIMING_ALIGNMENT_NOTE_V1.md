## Main practical conclusion
The timing problem is **not** evenly distributed.

The current preview is most compressed where the narration is densest:
- Beat 1 (opening hook)
- Beat 2 (first reveal)

And it is most generous where the narration is lightest:
- Beat 6 (closing memory)
- to a lesser extent Beat 4

So the next timing pass should probably behave less like “make everything longer” and more like:

1. **give the opening materially more room**
2. **give the first reveal more room**
3. **recover some of that time from the closing and possibly parts of the examples block**

## Best planning interpretation
If the working narration stays close to `SCRIPT_DRAFT_V2.md`, then:

- a narration-plausible version likely wants a total duration closer to roughly **179–192 seconds** than the current **171.84 seconds**
- the opening probably needs roughly **+12 to +14 seconds**
- the first reveal probably needs roughly **+16 to +18 seconds**
- the closing likely has enough room to give back roughly **10–11 seconds**
- Beat 4 may also be able to give back some time, depending on final narration delivery and motion design

## Important non-conclusion
This note does **not** justify changing the script right now.

The evidence points first to a **timing redistribution problem**, not necessarily a wording problem.
Broad script rewrites should remain frozen unless a later narration pass shows a narrower forced change.

## Recommended next step
When work resumes on this concept, the most useful next pass would be:

- a **timing redistribution experiment** guided by this note
- especially shifting time toward Beats 1–2
- while testing whether some time can be safely reclaimed from Beat 6 and possibly parts of Beat 4
